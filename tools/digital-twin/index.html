02 — The Tension

Complexity is a competitive advantage
— if you can see it.

Fragmented visibility
Truck, rail, ferry and customs live in separate systems. Deviations travel through inboxes — not dashboards. The full picture never exists in one place at one moment.
Reactive, not proactive
Decisions happen after the fact — a ferry delay, a customs hold, a rail issue. By the time the team reacts, the optimal rerouting window is already closed.
Data exists, insight doesn't
Years of operational data live in TMS, ERP and carrier feeds — but they're rarely used to simulate, predict or optimise. The gold is there. Nobody's mining it yet.
03 — The Idea

A Digital Twin
of ECS's supply chain.

01
Live network model
Every container, route, hub and warehouse — mirrored in real time. One source of truth that replaces a hundred spreadsheets and inbox threads. Every stakeholder sees the same picture, always.
02
What-if simulation
Test scenarios before reality hits. Ferry delayed? Rail at capacity? Customs hold expected? Run every fallback option in seconds — ranked by cost, ETA impact and sustainability.
03
AI-driven optimisation
Routes that cut empty kilometres. Mode choices that protect margin. Planning aligned with CSRD targets. The system learns from every decision and sharpens every week.

08 — Why Now

The window to build this
is open today.

Regulatory pressure
EU CSRD sustainability reporting goes live for mid-size firms in 2026. CO₂-per-shipment data will no longer be optional. ECS needs the data infrastructure now — not after the deadline, when every competitor is scrambling for the same solution.
AI tooling is finally mature
Two years ago this required a dedicated ML team and years of runway. Today, the combination of modern APIs, open-source simulation libraries and LLM-powered planning tools makes this buildable by a small, focused team inside 12 months.
Structural competitive moat
Every intermodal competitor is still reactive. The first operator to run a live digital twin builds a cost and service advantage that compounds every quarter — and becomes harder to copy as the model accumulates ECS-specific data.
